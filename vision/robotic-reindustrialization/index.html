Robotic Reindustrialization

Leading the global robotics revolution to reindustrialize Europe and create sustainable, localized manufacturing

The Robotics Revolution

Robotization is the new industrialization. To remain competitive globally, Europe must reinvent and reindustrialize itself as a robotized economy. This pillar focuses on creating an autonomous logistics network, developing robotic production capabilities, and building a sustainable, localized manufacturing ecosystem across the EU.

Robotics: The New Pension System

Embracing automation as a means to support an aging population, redefining social security for the 21st century. The robots will work for us, providing the foundation for social security systems in the AI era.

Pivoting from Automobiles to Robotics

Encouraging European industry, especially the German automotive sector, to lead in robotics platforms supply, enabling a new ecosystem for robotics startups and innovations in EU.

Logistical Integration

Overcoming Geographic Advantages

Addressing the unfair advantage of centrally-located countries by creating a unified, efficient logistics network across the EU. This levels the playing field for countries at the periphery.

European Unified Logistics Market

Creating a seamless transportation network enabling 48-hour delivery anywhere in the EU, leveling the playing field for e-commerce across the continent.

Autonomous Multimodal Transportation

Developing an integrated system of self-driving vehicles, drones, and smart infrastructure to revolutionize goods and people movement across Europe. This includes utilizing roads, railways, waterways, and even air routes to ensure efficient and sustainable transportation.

  • • Expanding the European high-speed rail network to allow overnight travel from any EU border to another, ensuring seamless connectivity.
  • • Ensuring that any package, regardless of size, ordered online from any location within the EU can be delivered to another location by the next day.
  • • The system will also focus on reusable packaging to minimize waste.

Infrastructure for the Robotic Age

Building Accessibility for Robots

Standardizing building designs and creating 3D models to facilitate robotic delivery and services, preparing our urban landscapes for an automated future.

Circular Economy Infrastructure

Autonomous logistics can significantly enhance recycling efforts by enabling the collection of even the smallest items for recycling. It can handle atypical items, allowing for the transport of hundreds of fractions over long distances.

Sustainable Agriculture

Autonomous Diverse Farming

Leveraging robotics and automation to create diverse, sustainable farming practices across Europe, increasing resilience and productivity.

Land Reserved for Natural Habitats

Setting aside permanent land reserves for natural ecosystems, ensuring biodiversity protection and environmental sustainability.

  • • New job opportunities in robotics
  • • Sustainable local production
  • • Enhanced quality of life

For Businesses

  • • Access to efficient logistics networks
  • • New markets for robotics innovation
  • • Reduced transportation costs
  • • Sustainable supply chains
  • • EU-wide customer reach

For Peripheral Regions

  • • Reduced geographic disadvantages
  • • New industrial opportunities
  • • Economic development
  • • Integration into EU economy

For Environment

  • • Enhanced recycling capabilities
  • • Reduced carbon footprint
  • • Sustainable agricultural practices
  • • Preservation of natural habitats
