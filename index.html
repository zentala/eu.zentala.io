Pillars of European Integration

I. Language Unification

Introducing English as a second official language across the EU. Every member state’s public administration becomes bilingual, sharing English as a common tongue.

II. Digital Administration Integration

Building a unified European digital government infrastructure instead of a tower of Babel. Leveraging technology to streamline and integrate administrative systems across the EU.

III. European Digital Public Services

Developing EU-wide digital services, such as a Predictive Healthcare System trained on pan-European data for early disease detection. Harnessing the power of big data and AI for the benefit of all Europeans.

IV. Logistical Integration

Creating a single, efficient logistics market in the EU. Ensuring packages reach any corner of Europe within 48 hours, fostering a truly interconnected continent.

I. Language Integration: English as the Lingua Franca of Europe

In European Politics

English as the Official Language of the EU

Imagine MEPs debating in a shared language, breaking down barriers and fostering true pan-European discourse.

English-Speaking European Media

Envision a vibrant landscape of European commentators discussing EU politics in English, creating a unified public sphere.

Cultivating European Political Awareness

Let’s tackle Europe’s challenges and shape its future together, freely exchanging ideas and perspectives in a common language.

Low Barrier to Entry

English is already widely adopted across the EU. Younger generations consume English content daily, and it’s the de facto language of international business in Europe.

In National States

'European English' as an Official Language

Every EU country becomes bilingual, with European English as a widely adopted, legally recognized, and practiced language alongside the national tongue.

Streamlined Bureaucracy

Handle your paperwork in English in any EU country, from tax forms to building permits.

Enhancing, Not Replacing

We’re not diminishing national languages, but adding a powerful tool for unity and efficiency.

English-Accessible Administration

From bilingual forms to English-speaking staff in every office, communicate with any government department in English.

A Gesture of Openness and Collaboration

Adopting English isn’t just for foreigners—it’s for all of us. It facilitates cooperation, opens doors to information, and embodies our belief in the power of unity and engagement.

II. Digital Administrative Integration

The Digital Government Revolution

Envisioning a future where every citizen, police officer, and judge interacts with the state through seamless digital systems. The government of tomorrow will be fully digital, paperless, and accessible through user-friendly interfaces.

Building a Unified Digital Europe

Instead of creating a digital Tower of Babel with each EU country digitalizing separately, let’s craft a shared vision for e-government applications. By building together, we can achieve economies of scale and true digital integration across the EU.

Collaborative Development, Individual Implementation

We can develop software collectively as EU countries, sharing an open-source approach, while allowing each nation to host and customize for its citizens. This ensures both unity in development and flexibility in implementation.

European Digital-First Governance

Creating a comprehensive digital system that prioritizes online interactions, making government services more efficient and accessible.

Code as Law

Recognizing computer code as a valid format for expressing and implementing laws, paving the way for more precise and automated governance.

Digital Direct Democracy

Leveraging digital systems to enable more direct forms of democracy, increasing citizen participation in decision-making processes.

Financial Reforms

Near-Zero Transaction Costs

Eliminating excessive fees for digital money transfers, treating financial transactions as a public service rather than a profit center for banks.

Digital Payment Zone

Creating a unified payment system across all 27 EU countries, facilitating seamless transactions regardless of local currency.

Automated Taxation

Designing a system where businesses can issue invoices and receive payments through a state-provided platform, with taxes automatically calculated and deducted.

Flexible Taxation for the Gig Economy

Implementing an adaptable tax system that accommodates the diverse and fluid nature of employment in the AI era.

Digital Receipts

Automatically generating digital receipts for all card payments, streamlining record-keeping for both consumers and businesses.

Overcoming Germany's Geographic Dividend

Addressing the unfair advantage of centrally-located countries by creating a unified, efficient logistics network across the EU.

Embracing automation as a means to support an aging population, redefining social security for the 21st century.

European Unified Logistics Market

Creating a seamless transportation network enabling 48-hour delivery anywhere in the EU, leveling the playing field for e-commerce across the continent.

Autonomous Multimodal Transportation

Developing an integrated system of self-driving vehicles, drones, and smart infrastructure to revolutionize goods and people movement across Europe.

Integration Principles

Seeking Mutual Benefit

Building a Common Culture

Using a shared language and media landscape to foster a truly European identity alongside national cultures.

Quality of Life Through Reform

Pursuing radical reforms and shifting competencies to the EU level to address challenges too big for individual nations, focusing on improving life for all Europeans rather than austerity.
