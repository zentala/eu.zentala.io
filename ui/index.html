    FAQ Section

    Common Questions

    Frequently Asked Questions

    Why do we need yet another European integration project?

    Unlike previous integration efforts, this vision focuses on practical, tangible benefits for all Europeans rather than abstract political concepts. By focusing on language, digital infrastructure, and robotic reindustrialization, we address the actual challenges facing Europe in an era of global competition and technological change.

    Isn't English as a second language unfair to non-English speaking countries?

    This proposal recognizes that English is already the de facto international language of business, science, and technology. Rather than starting from zero with a new language, we're acknowledging reality and formalizing it to reduce barriers. National languages remain primary, with English as a practical addition that enables greater European-wide communication and opportunity.

    How will these changes benefit ordinary citizens?

    Citizens will gain access to streamlined public services in multiple languages, enjoy faster cross-border shipping, benefit from enhanced AI-powered healthcare, access lifetime educational opportunities, and experience greater job mobility throughout Europe. The vision aims to create a Europe where everyday life becomes simpler, more efficient, and more prosperous for all.

    What if Germany or other powerful countries don't agree?

    If certain countries choose not to participate initially, this vision could begin as an Intermarium 2.0 project with countries like Poland, Ukraine, Romania, and Scandinavian nations. The benefits of integration would likely become apparent quickly, encouraging others to join. The proposal creates such clear mutual advantages that even skeptical countries would have strong incentives to participate.

    Why Digital Reform

    WhyDigitalReform Component Example

    1

    âš™ï¸ Adapting to a New Economic Era

    We already live in a digital and information-based economy, yet our administrative and state structures still operate with industrial-era logic. This fundamental misalignment hampers our potential.

    Instead of merely translating outdated administrative processes into technological solutions, we must redesign administrative processes from the ground up, optimizing for technology.

    Digital reform is not just digitization - it's a profound shift in how we think about the state and economy, treating them as a software project built from scratch with efficiency and modernity in mind.

    2

    ðŸ‡ªðŸ‡º Europe as a Unified Economic Organism

    To meet the great engineering and industrial challenges of our time, Europe must function as a unified economic organism. Only by working together at scale can we be competitive in the global economy.

    Linguistic and digital integration are the foundations that will enable functioning as a single organism - allowing us to effectively communicate and collaborate across borders.

    When we remove communication and bureaucratic barriers, we'll unleash Europe's immense creative and industrial potential, enabling ambitious continent-scale projects.

    3

    ðŸ’¶ The Economic Imperative

    Optimizing administrative, tax, and legal processes through digitization will radically transform the entire economy. Applying economies of scale and IT approaches to state management will bring enormous savings.

    Instead of 27 different administrative systems, we can build one shared system, pooling development and maintenance costs. This means better systems at lower costs.

    Transitioning from paper-based to digital processes is not just about convenience, but a fundamental shift in the efficiency of the entire economy.

    4

    ðŸ›¡ï¸ Defense and Security

    Robotization is an essential element of modern defense. Eastern European and Scandinavian countries, threatened by Russia, have a vital interest in deeply developing robotics for defensive purposes.

    Autonomous robotic systems can monitor borders, respond to threats, and provide effective deterrence, increasing the security of the entire Union.

    The same technology can help southern countries manage migration flows, offering humane and effective solutions for border patrol and monitoring.
