# Wprowadzenie języka angielskiego jako drugiego oficjalnego języka w każdym kraju UE 
...otworzyłoby osobom fizycznym i prawnym drzwi do wykonywania w dowolnym miejscu europy oficjalnych czynności prawnych w globalnie rozpoznawalnym języku. 

Takie działanie nie tylko manifestowałoby gościnność, ale i zwiększało otwartosc i dostępność naszych systemów administracji publicznej ekspatom, pracownikom migracyjnym oraz ponadnarodowemu biznesowi. 

Tworzyłoby to również korzystne warunki do ograniczenia monopoli, zwiększenia konkurencyjności, różnonodności, dostępności prduktów i uslug, stymulowania inwestycji miedzynarodowych w ramach UE oraz wzmacniania współpracy gospodarczej na szczeblu europejskim i ostatecznie facylitwoalby budowanie europejskiego biznesu. Stumulowalby to mobilnosc pracownicza.

Wprowadzenie angielskiego jako drugiego języka to praktyczny krok w kierunku federalizacji Europy, pozwalajacy kontynnowac integracje zachowujac jednocześnie pelnie autonomii państw członkowskich. Integracja językowa, opisana w tym artykule, umożliwiałaby osiągnięcie korzyści skali w ekonomii, ułatwiając rozwijanie przedsiębiorstw na skalę europejską oraz europejska integracje kulturowa ktora powinna doprowadzic do budowy wspolnej swiadomosci politycznej.

Ten krok otworzyłby nowy etap integracji europejskiej - integrację kulturową. Wspólny język przyczyniłby się do powstania europejskich firm medialnych, umożliwiłby naukę w renomowanych uczelniach w języku angielskim, rozwoj europejskiej nauki, facylitowal wszlekiego rodzaju wspolprace miedzynardowoa. Multum branz odniosłaby korzyści z oficjalnego ujednolicenia języka komunikacji w UE.

Wprowadzenie języka angielskiego jako drugiego oficjalnego języka w każdym kraju UE to format ktory to umozliwa. Na swiecie istnieje wiele panstw ktore sa wielojezykowe. Np w Singapurze mamy X jezykown naorodwch, ale rzad (zlozony glownie z chinczykow) obraduje po angielsku.

* (12 lat) Wprowadzenie obowiązku zaliczenia egzaminu z angielskiego na poziomie C1/C2 przed osiągnięciem pełnoletności.

# Introducing English as the Official Language of the European Union

In the contemporary European landscape, the role of English has become inseparable from its status as it becomed the de facto unofficial business language within the European Union. However, English is more than just a tool for work; it has also become the shared language of digital media consumption, with platforms like Netflix and Facebook. This underscores the current significance of English in the everyday lives of Europeans, both personally and professionally.

## Introducing English as the Second Official Language in Every EU Country

...would open the door for individuals and entities to conduct official legal activities across Europe in a globally recognized language.

This action would not only reflect european hospitality, but also enhance the openness and accessibility of our public administration systems to expatriates, migrant workers, and multinational businesses. 

It would create favorable conditions for limiting monopolies, boosting competitiveness, diversifying products and services, stimulating international investment within the EU, and strengthening economic cooperation at the European level, ultimately facilitating the growth of European businesses. It would also encourage labor mobility.

The introduction of English as a second language is a practical step toward the federalization of Europe, allowing for ongoing integration while preserving the full autonomy of member states. The language integration described in this article would enable economies of scale, facilitating the development of enterprises on a European scale, as well as cultural integration that would contribute to the construction of a shared political consciousness.

This step would mark a new phase of European integration - cultural integration. A common language would contribute to the emergence of European media companies, enable education in renowned institutions in English, advance European research, and facilitate all forms of international collaboration. Numerous industries would benefit from the official standardization of the communication language in the EU.

The introduction of English as a second official language in every EU country would pave the way for this. Many countries in the world are multilingual. For instance, in Singapore, there are various native languages, yet the government (largely composed mostly of Chinese speakers) conducts its proceedings in English.

In practice, the introduction of English as a second official language might follow a timeline like this:
* (2 years) All addresses have a corresponding code, eliminating the need to pronounce street names in a foreign language.
* (4 years) Museums and public places are labeled in English.
* (6 years) Official government forms are available in English.
* (8 years) xxx
* (10 years) English becomes a mandatory subject in school, from childhood.
* (12 years) Mandatory passing of an English examination at the C1/C2 level before adulthood.
* (14 years) Legal proceedings can be conducted in English.
* (16 years) Every public office has personnel proficient in English to assist with all matters.

## English as the Official Language of the European Union

Currently, Members of the European Parliament rely on translators to communicate with each other. This approach seems incongruent with the spirit of serious European integration and hampers authentic communication. Therefore, those engaged in this matter should converse in European English, and we should start cultivating an elite group capable of addressing the challenges and issues of Europe's future while communicating in the same language.

The introduction of English as a common language would fortify European integration, providing impetus for the further development of European identity and a shared political consciousness. Strengthened European identity would play a pivotal role in curbing the growth of Eurosceptic movements.

Moreover, it could open the EU to more robust integration with all Anglo-Saxon countries (UK, Ireland, US, Canada, New Zealand, Australia), thereby enhancing unity in the West. This would consolidate the European Union within the Anglo-Saxon realm, a realm we, as Poles, perceive as integral to our security. Concurrently, it would create conditions within the EU for gradual self-sufficiency and strategic independence over several generations.

And even if (knock on wood) the European Union were to dissolve someday, it would be easier to rebuild with a strong operational foundation in the form of a common language.
